## 2. UNIVERSAL PATTERNS EXTRACTED

### Pattern 1 — The Stack Deal (Fastpass / Bundle Bump)
Every successful funnel has a **bundle offer** either as a bump or early OTO that sells everything at a discount.
- ShopReelAI: $230 Fastpass (shown right after FE purchase — "if yes" path)
- Reddify: $317 Infinity Bundle (bump offer)
- Why it works: impatient buyers who know they want everything skip the individual pitch pages. Higher AOV, lower refund risk (they're committed).
- **Takeaway:** Always offer a bundle. Position it immediately after FE purchase.

### Pattern 2 — Unlimited Always Comes First
Every single funnel puts "Unlimited" (remove limits) as OTO 1.
- The FE always has artificial limits (credits, projects, campaigns, clients)
- OTO 1 removes those limits for a recurring fee
- This is the highest-converting OTO because the buyer just fell in love with the product and immediately hits a wall
- **Takeaway:** FE must have meaningful limits. OTO 1 removes them. Do not ship FE without limits.

### Pattern 3 — The Naming Ladder (Unlimited → Professional → Agency)
The naming follows a clear psychological ladder:
- **Unlimited** = power user, no restrictions
- **Professional** = serious/legit, branding control
- **Agency** = business builder, sell to clients
- Each name signals a different identity the buyer wants to adopt
- **Takeaway:** Name your OTOs after the identity, not the features.

### Pattern 4 — White Label Splits Across Two OTOs
All three funnels split white-label across two tiers:
- **Professional** = remove branding, add your own logo/colors (the cosmetic layer)
- **Agency** = client management + DFY website + sell as your own (the business layer)
- They are priced differently because the buyer is different:
  - Professional buyer = freelancer who wants to look good for clients
  - Agency buyer = entrepreneur who wants to build a business
- **Takeaway:** Never bundle "remove our logo" with "manage 100 clients" — they are different value propositions.

### Pattern 5 — DFY is Always a Service OTO, Not a Software OTO
In all three funnels, DFY means a human does something for you:
- Reddify: team sets up your campaigns
- ShopReelAI: team configures accounts, onboards first 5 clients, builds 90-day content calendar
- AIDirectors: team configures platform, creates avatars, installs scripts, 2-hour live training
- Price range: $147–$197 one-time (never recurring)
- It's always one-time because it's a service, not ongoing software
- **Takeaway:** DFY = your time. High margin, no code. Offer it. Limit slots if needed.

### Pattern 6 — Agency OTO is the Workhorse
Agency is where the real money is and where the most value is packed:
- ShopReelAI Agency ($197/y): 100 client accounts + white-label + DFY reseller license + DFY agency website + hosting + Stripe/PayPal integration + client reports
- AIDirectors Agency ($57/y): 100 clients + white-label + DFY website + team seats + approval workflows + 5 years hosting + contracts + booking system
- Notice: Agency always includes a DFY website/sales page — this is the "promo site" for white-label buyers
- **Takeaway:** Agency OTO should feel like a complete business-in-a-box.

### Pattern 7 — Pricing Psychology
| OTO type | Price range | Billing |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Unlimited | $67–97 | Yearly (recurring) |
| Professional | $47–67 | Yearly (recurring) |
| Agency | $57–197 | Yearly (recurring) |
| DFY | $147–197 | One-time |
| White Label / Reseller | $97–497 | One-time |
| Bundle | $197–317 | One-time |

- Recurring OTOs = software features (justified as ongoing access)
- One-time OTOs = services, rights, or physical deliverables
- **Takeaway:** Don't charge recurring for things that don't have ongoing cost.

### Pattern 8 — The "if yes / if no" Fork (ShopReelAI)
ShopReelAI showed the fork explicitly in their JV page funnel diagram:
- After FE: offer the Fastpass bundle
- If YES → DFY upsell (they're a serious buyer, go deeper)
- If NO → individual OTOs (Unlimited → Professional → Agency)
- This maximises revenue from both buyer types
- **Takeaway:** Build the fork. High-intent buyers get the bundle path. Price-sensitive buyers get the step-up path.

## 3. WHAT EVERY WINNING AGENCY OTO INCLUDES

Cross-referencing all three funnels, a winning Agency OTO always has:

- [ ] Client sub-accounts (50–100 clients)
- [ ] Full white-label (logo, colors, domain)
- [ ] DFY promotional/agency website template
- [ ] Client management dashboard
- [ ] Team seats / VA access
- [ ] Payment integration (Stripe/PayPal)
- [ ] Client reports / ROI dashboard
- [ ] DFY legal templates (contracts, NDAs)
- [ ] Years of hosting included OR custom domain support

Not all products have all of these — but the ones with more win more.

## 5. OWNER PLAN (Internal — Never Public)

A hidden super-plan assigned directly in the DB. Not on the pricing page, not in the app UI.

### What it is
- `plan = 'owner'` in the entitlements table
- Bypasses every single limit check in the backend
- No feature is gated — full access to everything including future AI coins
- Assignable by Chike manually to any workspace

### Who gets it
- Chike's own workspace — always
- Anyone Chike explicitly grants it to (team, partners, beta testers)

### How it works technically
- When limits system is built, every check starts with:
  `if plan == 'owner': return # no limits, skip`
- Never surfaced in frontend — no badge, no label, nothing visible
- Assigned via direct DB insert into `entitlements` table

### DB row
```sql
INSERT INTO entitlements (workspace_id, product_id, plan, source, status)
VALUES ('<workspace_id>', 'pagepersona', 'owner', 'internal', 'active');
```

## 6. AI LAYER — PLANNED (Post-Manual Build)

### Philosophy
Finish the full manual product first. Then audit every feature for where AI adds genuine value. AI is woven in, not bolted on as a separate tool.

### Monetisation Model — AI Coins
- Every workspace gets a coins allocation based on their plan
- AI actions cost coins (e.g. generate a rule = 10 coins, write personalised copy = 5 coins)
- When coins run out, the feature is locked until they recharge
- Coins can be rebought at any time — this is the ongoing revenue layer beyond OTO purchases
- Owner plan = unlimited coins always

### Coin Allocation by Plan (draft — finalise after AI audit)
| Plan | Monthly Coins | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| FE Core | 50 | Enough to try, not enough to rely on |
| Unlimited | 200 | Power user allocation |
| Professional | 200 | Same as Unlimited |
| Agency | 500 | Covers their clients too |
| Owner | Unlimited | No coins deducted ever |

### Coin Recharge Packs (standalone purchases — not OTOs)
| Pack | Coins | Price |
|------|-------|-------|
| Starter | 100 | $7 |
| Growth | 500 | $27 |
| Pro | 2,000 | $67 |
| Agency | 10,000 | $197 |

### Where AI Will Be Audited For (post-build)
- Rule suggestions based on traffic patterns
- AI-written personalised copy for swap_text
- Popup headline and CTA generation
- Analytics insights ("here's what your rules are telling you")
- Auto-rule creation from page scan

### Build Order
1. Finish all manual features completely
2. Audit every screen — identify where AI adds value
3. Design coin costs per action
4. Build AI layer with coin deduction middleware
5. Add coin recharge via Stripe

## 7. WISDOM SUMMARY (TL;DR)

1. **FE must have limits** — no limits = no OTO 1
2. **Unlimited OTO always first** — highest converter
3. **Professional = cosmetic white-label** (remove branding)
4. **Agency = business white-label** (sell to clients)
5. **DFY = your time, not code** — one-time, high margin, limited slots
6. **Bundle/Fastpass = serve the "just give me everything" buyer** — always offer it
7. **Agency OTO should feel like a complete business-in-a-box**
8. **Recurring = software features. One-time = rights, services, deliverables**
9. **Name OTOs after the identity the buyer wants to become**
10. **The fork (if yes / if no) maximises revenue from both buyer types**
